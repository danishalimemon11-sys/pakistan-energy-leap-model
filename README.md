# Strategizing for Carbon Neutrality: Pakistan Energy Sector Analysis
### LEAP-Based Policy Scenario Modelling | 2022–2040

Long-term energy system modelling of Pakistan's electricity sector 
using the LEAP (Low Emissions Analysis Platform) framework, 
evaluating three policy scenarios for achieving net-zero emissions 
by 2040.

---

## Overview

Pakistan's energy sector faces critical challenges — heavy fossil fuel 
dependency, rising GHG emissions, and energy affordability gaps. This 
study develops a national energy model to evaluate decarbonization 
pathways across three scenarios from 2022 to 2040.

---

## Scenarios

| Scenario | Description | Key Technology |
|----------|-------------|----------------|
| Reference | Business as usual | Fossil fuel dominated |
| WEM | With Existing Measures | Energy efficiency technology |
| WAM | With Additional Measures | 80% renewable energy mix |

---

## Key Results

| Metric | Reference | WEM | WAM |
|--------|-----------|-----|-----|
| GHG Emissions 2040 | 345.3 Mt | 289.1 Mt | ~0 Mt |
| Emission Reduction | — | 16.3% | ~100% |
| Total Capacity 2040 | 304.7 GW | 231.5 GW | 312.5 GW |
| Production Cost 2040 | $66.3B | $55.0B | $73.6B |
| Renewable Share 2040 | 29.3% | 29.3% | ~80% |

- WAM scenario achieves net-zero by 2040 with 131 GW wind + 
  60.3 GW solar
- WEM reduces capital costs by 14.8% and variable costs by 24.2%
- WAM requires 73.2 GW less capacity than reference scenario

---

## Methodology

- National GHG inventory model per IPCC 2006 Guidelines
- Demand projections based on GDP (6%), population growth (1.7%)
- Three policy intervention scenarios modelled in LEAP
- Assessment across GHG emissions, energy security, and energy equity
